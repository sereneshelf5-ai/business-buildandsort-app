Project Brief: Business Builder App
One-line pitch
An app where a user enters a business idea and a problem it solves, and the app walks them step-by-step through building that business — from figuring out who needs it, to marketing it, to testing a working prototype.
How it works (high level)
	1.	User inputs their business idea, the problem it solves, and any known details (target customer, budget, etc.)
	2.	The app takes that input and walks the user through a series of stages, one at a time
	3.	At each stage, the app generates specific, usable output the user can act on or test
	4.	The user can move forward, go back, or restart with a new business idea at any time
	5.	Multiple business ideas can be saved and revisited separately
Stages
Stage 1: Needs
Goal: Confirm the problem is real and worth solving.
What the app does: Analyzes the idea and problem statement, identifies whether it’s addressing a real need, and highlights gaps or risks.
What the user sees: A summary of the core need, why it matters, and any red flags to consider before moving forward.
Stage 2: Audience
Goal: Define who actually has this problem.
What the app does: Builds one or more buyer personas based on the idea (demographics, motivations, pain points, buying behavior).
What the user sees: Clear persona profiles they can compare and pick from, plus guidance on which audience segment to prioritize.
Stage 3: Marketing
Goal: Figure out how to reach and message that audience.
What the app does: Suggests positioning, messaging angles, and channels (e.g. social media, local outreach, marketplaces) tailored to the chosen persona.
What the user sees: A short marketing plan — key message, target channels, and example content/copy they could use immediately.
Stage 4: Prototype
Goal: Give the user something real to test.
What the app does: Generates a lightweight prototype concept — could be a mockup, a landing page draft, a sample product description, or a simple test plan.
What the user sees: A tangible “thing” to try out in the real world, plus suggested ways to test it (e.g. show it to 5 people, post it, see who responds) and how to interpret the results.
Cross-cutting features
	•	User can save multiple business ideas and switch between them
	•	Each stage’s output is saved so the user can revisit or edit it later
	•	Simple, guided UI — one stage at a time, clear “next” and “back” navigation
	•	Eventually: ability to export the full plan (needs + audience + marketing + prototype) as a single document
First build target (MVP)
A basic web app with:
	•	A homepage where the user enters their business idea and problem
	•	One working stage (start with “Needs”) that takes that input and returns a generated analysis
	•	Simple navigation to move to the next stage (can be a placeholder for now)
